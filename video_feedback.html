Student-Teacher Project Feedback Loop

UX/UI for eSpark Learning

Performance tasks are asked of students at the end of each quest in eSpark to show a deeper understanding of the skill they have just practiced. To truly bring value to this feature, there needed to be a way for teachers to give quick and meaningful feedback.

We noticed that students were making teach-back videos of low quality (as in, students answered custom prompts incorrectly or inaudibly), and there was no easy way for teachers to access their students' work.

We examined what we called the "Cycle of Poorly Made Student Videos" to determine that teachers needed direct ways to find and give feedback on students' projects in order for quality to improve.

This is the first time we implemented a direct in-app loop of communication between student and teacher. The flow had to be simple and discoverable so that we don't end up having broken links, and subsequently perpetuate the cycle of poorly made student videos.

This feature was launched and rolled out to all partners in November of 2015. Teachers receive direct links to recently submitted student videos on a weekly basis. Teachers can quickly give student projects a rating out of four stars, accompanied by an optional custom comment.

Students see personalized feedback in their own app environment and can return to review it at any time.

Since launch, over 32,000 student videos have been graded and given individual, meaningful feedback. The goal is to ultimately see improvement in the quality of student submissions, whether that be in presentation skills or mastery of a skill.
When we look at a comparison from the early release to a more recent time, we can see a rightward shift in earned star ratings. There was a 10% increase in percentage of 4 star ratings earned by student projects in the latter end of the observation cycle.

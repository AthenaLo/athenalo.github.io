The Big Easy

Furniture Design

There is a science and algorithm of sorts to creating comfort within furniture, especially when the bodies come in various shapes and sizes. This chair is an exploration of ergonomics to a very specific end user.

There is an existing formula for the average human body; ideally, humans are is about seven to eight heads tall, and everything is proportioned out from there. The abundantly clear issue is that no one is the ideal, and the term "average" hardly summarizes the spectrum of body types.

Acting as the model for this study in ergonomics, I had to keep in mind the following points: I am shorter than the average person (5'2"), with a longer torso to leg ratio than most, and am, in fact, 6.5 heads tall.

After playing around with inexpensive life sized mockups to feel out the ideal angles, heights, and depths to achieve comfort, measurements were translated into compound curves to be CNC-ed for the seat and the back. The arms and legs were built through a very controlled process of bent lamination.
The Big Easy currently resides at my parents' home in New York, where people of like size and proportion to me enjoy it every day.

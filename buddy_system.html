The Buddy System

Product UI/UX Design

Diagnoses of childhood ADHD is steadily increasing year by year and classrooms are becoming more difficult to manage, while students are struggling to get the individual attention they need. Tara Hosseinipour and I designed a system to explore how seamless, personalized classroom management, as well as individualized goal setting, can have an impact on learning and self esteem.
We partnered with a local Rhode Island school that focuses on serving students with special needs by working together to set personal goals and providing strategies to develop self confidence. Through observation and research, we identified how we can adapt this routine into a product and system design.

Tara and I devised a three-part system that marries an app and dashboard for teachers with a discrete device for students that encourages interactions with a UI and tactile components. This system is built on the concepts of individualized goal-setting, instantaneous feedback, and ownership over one's progress.

RISD's Industrial Design Department the 2012 Alexander Graham Bell Award for social impact, specifically regarding young learners and play

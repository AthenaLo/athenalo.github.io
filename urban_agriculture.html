Urban Agriculture Exhibit

Exhibit/Communication Design

As students at RISD, we were asked to design exhibits that create an educational experience around an environmental or social issue. My group elected to build an immersive exhibit about how urban agriculture, and supporting local initiatives, is an easy way to make a positive impact on food culture, as well as the environment.

With bloating city populations, more and more produce gets imported from faraway farms to feed the masses. A consumer assumption, additionally, is that when goods are labeled "organic," they're inherently better in quality and wholly.

There is a better way to get fresh food, even in an urban environment. Our aim for the exhibit was to communicate and convince our audience of the advantages of supporting community gardens.

We researched the detrimental processes around how our nearest grocery store, Whole Foods, obtains their produce, as well as the community building and environmental aspects of local urban gardens.

After visiting a Providence community garden, we were determined to bring the rustic, earthy feeling to our exhibition space to share with our audience.

Our urban agriculture exhibit focused on communicating the differences in carbon footprint, as well as freshness, of locally sourced and Whole Foods produce. We sought to engage memory, forward thinking, as well as taste through informative signs, takeaway seeds, and apples to taste test (to compare apples to apples, if you will).

At the end of the department exhibition, we donated our exhibit to a local community garden in Providence to continue educating the public about the benefits of urban agriculture and how to make ecological conscious choices.
